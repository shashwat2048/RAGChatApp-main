Q: What is a Chartered Accountant?
A: A Chartered Accountant (CA) is a professional accountant qualified to handle various financial aspects of businesses including auditing, taxation, financial reporting, and business advisory services.

Q: What are the main responsibilities of a CA?
A: Key responsibilities include financial statement preparation and analysis, tax planning and compliance, auditing, management consulting, and ensuring regulatory compliance.

Q: How does one become a Chartered Accountant?
A: To become a CA, one typically needs to complete a bachelor's degree, pass the CA entrance exam, complete articleship training, and clear the final CA examination.

Q: What is the difference between a CA and a CPA?
A: While both are accounting professionals, CAs are typically trained under Commonwealth systems with focus on auditing and law, while CPAs are more common in the US system with focus on US GAAP and taxation.

Q: What skills are important for a CA?
A: Important skills include analytical thinking, attention to detail, knowledge of accounting standards, communication skills, and ethical judgment.
